Apply to join the register of apprenticeship training providers (RoATP)

Find out how to become an apprenticeship training provider so that your can train apprentices in England.

Contents

  • Who can apply
  • What you’ll need
  • Apply
  • What happens next
  • Removal from the RoATP

Once your organisation’s on the RoATP, it will be:

  1. listed on the register of apprenticeship training providers
  2. listed on find an apprenticeship
  3. eligible to receive government funding to train apprentices

Being on the RoATP does not mean your organisation is recommended by Education and Skills Funding Agency (ESFA). It cannot use ESFA as a ‘kite-mark’ or an award and will not have an identification number for the RoATP.

Existing apprenticeship training providers

If your organisation is invited to reapply or been unsuccessful using the Department for Education portal (Bravo), you’ll need to apply as an existing apprenticeship training provider.

Who can apply

Organisations must apply through one of 3 provider routes.

Main provider route

This route allows your organisation to directly deliver training to apprentices for other organisations. While doing this, it can also train its own employees, employees of connected companies or charities or act as a subcontractor for other main and employer providers.

Your organisation must have at least 12 months’ trading history and a training manager with at least 9 months’ experience in developing and delivering training.

Employer provider route

This route allows your organisation to directly deliver training to its own employees. It can also train employees of connected companies or charities or act as a subcontractor for other employer or main providers.

Your organisation must:

  • be a levy-paying employer
  • have at least 12 months’ trading history
  • have a training manager with at least 9 months’ experience in developing and delivering training

Supporting provider route

This route allows your organisation to act as a subcontractor for apprenticeship training to main and employer providers. It will train apprentices up to a maximum of £500,000 per year. This is limited to £100,000 for your organisation’s first year on the RoATP.

Your organisation must have at least 3 months’ trading history and a training manager with at least 3 months’ experience in developing and delivering training.

What you’ll need

Make sure your organisation has:

  1. a sign in account (if you do not have one, you can create one when you apply)
  2. a UK provider reference number (UKPRN)
  3. an Information Commissioner's Office registration number (ICO number)
  4. a UK business address

Depending on your organisation and provider route, you may need to know:

  1. who’s in control of your organisation and their dates of birth
  2. your organisation’s financial figures
  3. if your organisation or anyone who’s in control has had any criminal convictions in the last 3 years and what they are
  4. how your organisation manages its relationship with employers and subcontractors
  5. who’s in your organisation’s management hierarchy for apprenticeships
  6. the sectors your organisation will offer apprenticeships in
  7. how your organisation plans to deliver apprenticeship training
  8. who will deliver apprenticeship training - including any sector specific experience and qualifications they might have
  9. how your organisation will evaluate training

Depending on your organisation and provider route, you may need to upload:

  1. financial evidence covering the last 12 months
  2. a continuity plan for apprenticeship training
  3. an equality and diversity policy
  4. a safeguarding policy
  5. a Prevent duty policy
  6. a health and safety policy
  7. a complaints policy
  8. a contract for services template with employers
  9. a commitment statement template
  10. a policy for professional development of employees

Apply to join the register

Apply using the digital application to join the RoATP.

You must answer the first section in order as it determines the rest of the application questions. Once you do this, you can answer the rest of the application in any order.

We’ll guide you through the application and you’ll be able to ask for help on each page.

You do not have to complete your application in one go. You can save your details and finish it later.

As part of making an application, your organisation must agree to the terms of conditions of joining the RoATP.

What happens next

You’ll need to allow at least 12 weeks for ESFA to process your application.

ESFA will:

  1. do checks on your application and the people involved in your organisation
  2. tell you if your application has been successful or unsuccessful

ESFA may also ask for further information on any application answers (they may withdraw your application if you do not comply).

You’ll receive an email when there’s an update with your application.

Check the progress of your application.

If your application is successful, you’ll receive confirmation from ESFA which will include your next steps.

If your application is unsuccessful

If your application is unsuccessful, you’ll receive confirmation from ESFA including how you can appeal the decision or try again.

Your organisation can only apply twice in a 12-month period.

Change or withdraw your application

You can change your application at any time before ESFA has given their decision.

To make a change or withdraw your application you can email: XXXXX

Make a complaint

To make a complaint, use ESFA’s complaints procedure.

Removal from the RoATP

Apprenticeship training providers are removed from the RoATP if they no longer meet ESFA’s requirements. Check if your organisation no longer meets ESFA’s requirements.
